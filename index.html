SONAR

Sonar is a way to connect with those around you and keep one another informed about the community and its issues. We’re all in this together.

  • Learn more

Why we formed

Local issues affect our day to day lives too much to ignore, but people are unable to access this information with massive amounts of local newspapers shutting down.

  • Learn more

Why that's important

By giving people easier access to information at local levels, they can make the changes they want to see happen- or at the very least be informed.

  • Learn more

How will we do this?

By crowdsourcing information from citizens, journalists, and public safety officials we can make our communities better places.

  • Learn more

What we will achieve

Here's what Sonar will bring to the table:

Preventing extreme bias

While it is natural for news to be biased and pander to its audience, it creates a tribal mentality of “us vs. them”.

Increasing access to local news

Local newspapers are being bought out or driven out of business. However, people have more power at the local level and can make a difference

Reducing social media as a source for news

Most people turn to social media like Facebook and Twitter for their news. Social media keeps users in bubbles and feeds them articles that they’re most likely to engage with (both positively and negatively)

Reducing corporate influence

News outlets such as CNN and Fox are corporations with their own agendas. As public companies, they have an obligation to shareholders. However, they have the power to shape public opinion on war, social issues, and others.

Diversifying perspectives

Just like how Yelp allowed us to get multiple perspectives on a restaurant rather than just one food critic’s review, we get our news straight from the source rather than a journalist handpicking quotes from their sources. .

Keeping eachother safe

With real-time updates on public safety we can warn one another when the unimaginable happens.
